Countries With Billionares.

A billionaire is defined as a person whose net worth is more than 1 billion. Anyone whose net worth is above a billion is already considered one of the wealthiest in the world. Jeff Bezos, the founder and CEO of Amazon is the world richest man with a net worth of more than 100 billion.

Currently, there are 74 countries with billionaires in the world. There are 2604 billionaires according to Wealth-X Billionaire Census 2019 where most of them are from the United States of America with 705 out of the total 2604.

China is the second country with the highest number of billionaires with 285 of them. Germany, Russia, and the United Kingdom join them in the top five. The total net worth of billionaires is more than 9.5 trillion.

As of 2019, the world youngest billionaire was Kyle Jenner who by then was only 21 years old. She is an influencer with investments in the make up industry. She is the sister of Kim Kardashian. Another young billionaires of the world include John Collision who is now 29, the founder of online payment gateway stripe. Also, Evan Spiegel (age 28), co-founder, and CEO of Snapchat.

To become this wealthy is not easy. Most of the world millionaires and billionaires share characteristics that show to be rich acquiring wealth requires a lot of hard work. They are usually knowledgeable on their fields. They are also known to be risk takers.

This usually not the case for all of them, some have acquired wealth through inheritance. A good example is Laurene Powell Jobs, who was the wife of Steve Jobs who acquired the wealth after Steve Job passed on. Her net worth currently stands at $14.4 billion.

Others such as Bill Gate and Mark Zuckerberg gained their wealth from the technology industry. Most like Micheal Bloomberg, Oprah Winfrey etc gained their wealth from their businesses. It should however be noted that this billionaires spend a huge part of their wealth on donations.
