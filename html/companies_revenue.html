World's Highest Revenue Ever Reported By Companies.

A company's revenue, also known as turnover, refers to income generated by a company in it various business activity, ie income from services rendered and goods bought by its customers. This activities include sales of it primary and secondary products and services, investments activities which provide royalties, discounts, rents, dividends, interests and other incomes. A company's revenue is measured periodically. The period for most companies is usually one year.

Revenue amount for different companies vary significantly. Revenue for companies is usually found on the first line of their profit and loss account. Sales revenue is the main revenue for many companies. It is calculated by multiplying the number of unit sold with the selling price.

Revenue of a company is affected by many factors. The main factor goes without saying is the market size. Some companies such as Walmart have enormous revenues because of their market size. Another big factor that affect the revenues of a company is ownership. A company like Saudi Aramco is a good example for this factor, it is owned by Saudi Arabia Government which means resources required by the company are easily obtained.

Another big factor is the type of goods and services provided by a company. Companies in the oil business tend to have a high revenue because of the high demand of their products world wide. That why most of the companies in the below list are in the oil and petroleum industry.

Another factor is leadership. Companies with visionary leaders such as Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Berkshire Hathaway etc who have guided their companies into big success. Steve Jobs for example, with his innovation lead Apple to have huge revenues generated from it's innovative Apple products.

When the above factors are present, revenue is affected positively and if there are inadequate, revenue is affected negatively. It is important for companies to ensure factors that affect revenue positively are available to ensure growth of a company.
