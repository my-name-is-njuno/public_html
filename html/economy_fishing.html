The World Biggest Fishing Producers.

Fishing is the activity of catching fish to feed on from water bodies like rivers, oceans, lakes, ponds etc. Fishing also may include catching aquatic animals other than fish such as crabs whales etc. People who practice fishing are known as fishermen. More than 20% meat eaten by people in the world belongs is from fishing activities.

Many countries in the world staple food is fish. Other countries depend on fish exports as one of their source of income. Some people in developed countries, fishing is a recreational activity.

Aquaculture on the other hand involves breeding, rearing, and harvesting of fish for feeding, crustaceans, shellfish, algae, and other organisms in all types of water environments mostly in ponds. This type of activity is done in controlled spaces. The difference between fishing and aquaculture is that for fishing, fish are caught in the wild while aquaculture is usually fish ponds.

There are many techniques of fishing. This including trapping, netting, the use of a spear, hand gathering etc. Recreational fishers fish for pleasure, sport, or to provide food for themselves, while commercial fishers to fish for profit. Artisanal fishers use traditional, low-tech methods, for survival in third-world countries, and as a cultural heritage in other countries. Usually, recreational fishers use angling methods and commercial fishers use netting.

The main sources of wild fish in the world are lakes, oceans, and rivers. Water is the natural habitat of fish. Water covers more than 70% of the world land area. Thus meaning there is plenty of fish.

The main challenge being faced by the fishing industry is overfishing. This is where fish are caught in huge volumes leading to decrease of fish population in huge numbers. The techniques being used in fishing and the fishing gears are the main problem that is causing this issue. Most of them end up even destroying the fish habitat.
